Common Abbreviations and Meanings

  • BCI: Brain-Computer Interface - A direct communication pathway between the brain and an external device.
  • EEG: Electroencephalography - A method to record electrical activity of the brain using electrodes placed on the scalp.
  • ERP: Event-Related Potential - A measured brain response resulting from a specific sensory, cognitive, or motor event.
  • SSVEP: Steady-State Visually Evoked Potential - A visual response of the brain that's elicited by visual stimuli flickering at a constant frequency.
  • P300: A positive ERP component that occurs around 300 milliseconds after a stimulus, commonly used in BCI applications.
  • MI: Motor Imagery - A process of imagining a motor movement without actually performing the movement, often used in BCI paradigms.
  • NFB: Neurofeedback - A kind of biofeedback using real-time EEG data.
  • ICA: Independent Component Analysis - A computational method for separating independent sources from mixed signals, often used to remove artifacts from EEG data.
